I'm getting a message about the Vive Camera needing to be enabled.
  • The camera on your HTC Vive HMD may be disabled. In the SteamVR desktop app menu, click Settings > Camera > Enable Camera.

  • Sometimes, the HTC Vive camera is enabled but not available. Restarting SteamVR or disconnecting/reconnecting the Vive HMD camera usually works for us, but check SteamVR Troubleshooting for other, more up-to-date solutions.

  • Close Bridge SW (System Tray > Right Click Bridge Icon > Quit) and restart.
How can I re-run the Bridge Setup?
The guided Bridge VR setup can be re-run at any time via the ‘Configure Bridge in VR’ button on the Settings tab of this Bridge desktop app.
How can I pair my HTC Vive Tracker to track my keyboard?
  • First your Vive Tracker must be paired with your HTC Vive system (SteamVR beta Menu > Devices > Pair Controller).

  • Next, in the Bridge desktop application, go to the Pair Vive Tracker tab, and once your tracker appears on the list, click on Pair New + and shake the desired tracker for a few seconds.

My tracker is not appearing on the list.
Make sure it is already paired with SteamVR (SteamVR beta Menu > Devices > Pair Controller): check that it is switched on and the LED is lit GREEN. If your tracker is still not appearing on the list, please contact us.
I'm not seeing a keyboard in VR.
  • Check that the Keyboard Visibility Toggle (labelled ‘Enable Keyboard’) in the Bridge app Settings tab is set to ‘On’.

  • If you still cannot see a keyboard, or are seeing an object that doesn’t look like a keyboard, please contact us.

I’ve completed the Bridge VR Setup, and I’m still not getting a clear image of my hands.
As far as possible, we recommend using Bridge in uniform lighting conditions. We advise against harsh lights or light at unusual angles, as reflections and glare negatively affect the experience. From our research, when used in ideal lighting conditions, there should always be a clear hand image, but in non-ideal lighting conditions, a low contrast between keyboard color and hand skin tone can result in a poor experience. We are aware of this, and it’s the primary issue that we are working to solve today: experimenting with different lighting, differently colored keyboards (we’ve been experimenting with blue prototype G810s!), matt finishes and more.
I can see colored dots (‘noise’), the same color as my virtual hands, on my virtual keyboard.
This can happen as we use luminance and color space to filter your real hands as separate from your real keyboard. Adjust the Hand Visibility slider in Settings.
My hands are not aligned with the virtual keyboard.
On your Bridge VR keyboard, hold the ALIGN key to bring you into Alignment mode. The goal is to use your mouse to align your real keyboard, viewed through the camera-feed, with the blue key outlines. For more information, check out the Controls section of the Settings tab.
My keyboard “swims” - what can I do?
When you move your head, the keyboard and hands may appear to swim or move in front of you in the virtual space. This is due to a delay between the acquisition of the pose of the keyboard from the tracker and the rendering of both layers (keyboard and hands) in our engine. We realise it can be somewhat disconcerting for some people, and we’re working on improving this for the next release.
